The Effects of Climate Change

The potential future effects of global climate change include more wildfires, longer periods of heat in some places and an increase of tropical storms. Climate change has had many effects on the environment. Glaciers have shrunk, ice on rivers and lakes have melted faster, and plants and animals have moved. Also, loss of sea ice, accelerated sea level rise and longer, and more intense heat waves.

  • Climate change will continue

    • Global climate is going to continue to change over this century and further.

  • Artic likely to become ice-free

    • The Artic Ocean is expected to become ice free in summer before mid-century.

  • More droughts and heat waves

    • Droughts and heat waves everywhere are going to become more intense and cold waves less intense.

  • Hurricanes

    • Hurricanes will become stronger and more intense.
